Who We Are

About Us

The Bookmarked Life is a free and open library dedicated to fostering lifelong learning and curiosity. We curate a diverse collection of books and resources, making knowledge accessible to everyone, everywhere.

Our Mission

We democratize access to information and stories, empowering individuals to explore, learn, and grow. Literature and knowledge should be freely available, breaking down barriers to education and personal development.

As a community-driven initiative, we rely on passionate volunteers and contributors who share our vision for open access and inclusivity.

Help Us Grow

The Bookmarked Life thrives thanks to volunteers who believe in the transformative power of reading. Join us in one of the following ways:

  • Developers: Enhance our platform, add new features, and improve user experience.
  • Content Curators: Discover and add new books, ensuring our library remains diverse and relevant.
  • Community Managers: Grow our presence on social media, engage with readers, and build a vibrant community.
  • Translators: Help us reach a global audience by translating content into different languages.

You can also support us by sharing The Bookmarked Life with your network, inviting others to explore and contribute.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: September 11, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information when you use our website and related services. By using the site you accept the practices described in this policy.

1. Controller

For the purposes of applicable data protection laws, the data controller is the operator of this website. If you have questions, requests, or complaints about how your data is handled, please contact us at infothebookmarkedlife@gmail.com.

2. Definitions

Personal data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable person. Processing means any operation performed on personal data (collecting, storing, using, sharing, deleting).

3. Information we collect

  • Telegram bot interaction data — temporary, anonymous identifiers and message content needed to fulfill the request. We do not link this identifier to other personal data or retain it beyond the time necessary to fulfill the request, unless you explicitly provide additional details.
  • Voluntary submissions — information you supply through third-party forms (e.g., contact forms, donations). These third parties may collect and store your data under their own policies.
  • Cookies & similar technologies — this site does not use analytics cookies for tracking by default. If we introduce cookies in future, we will disclose them and provide an opt-out mechanism.

4. Legal bases for processing

Where applicable (for users covered by laws such as the EU GDPR), we rely on the following lawful bases:

  • Consent — where you voluntarily provide personal data (e.g., contact forms, donations).
  • Contractual necessity — to perform services you requested (e.g., fulfill a donation or contact request).
  • Legitimate interests — for security, abuse prevention, and basic site operation, balanced against user rights.
  • Legal obligation — where required by law to preserve records or respond to legal process.

5. Data subject rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have some or all of the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • Right to access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (fix inaccurate data).
  • Right to erasure (delete) where legal grounds permit.
  • Right to restriction of processing.
  • Right to data portability where technically feasible.
  • Right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.

To exercise any right, contact us at infothebookmarkedlife@gmail.com. We will respond within the shorter of the timeframes required by applicable law or within 30 calendar days. If we need more time, we will inform you and explain why.

6. Children’s privacy

The site is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors under 13. If you believe we have collected such information, contact us to request deletion. Note: U.S. law (COPPA) treats under-13s as a protected group and requires parental consent for data collection in many cases.

7. Sharing & third parties

We do not sell personal data. We may share data with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf (hosting, forms, donation processors). Those providers are contractually bound to handle data securely and only for purposes we authorize.

8. International transfers

If personal data are transferred out of your jurisdiction (e.g., servers or processors outside Sri Lanka or the EU), we will protect such transfers using lawful mechanisms (adequacy decision, standard contractual clauses or other safeguards) as required by applicable law.

9. Data retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purpose collected, statutory obligations, or to resolve disputes. Typical retention periods:

  • Contact form submissions: up to 2 years unless you request deletion.
  • Donation records (for tax/financial recordkeeping): up to 7 years or as required by law.

10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect data (HTTPS, limited access, regular backups). No system is 100% secure; if a breach occurs we will follow applicable law for notification and remediation.

11. Breach notification

If a personal data breach occurs that poses a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, we will promptly notify the relevant supervisory authority, and where possible, within the timelines required by applicable laws (for instance, within 72 hours under GDPR and in accordance with the notification requirements of Sri Lanka’s PDPA). We will also inform affected individuals when mandated by law.

12. Your choices and opt-outs

You may opt out of future communications or withdraw consents by contacting us at infothebookmarkedlife@gmail.com. For third-party services (payment providers, Google Forms, Buy Me a Coffee) please review and use their opt-out settings.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. The “Last updated” date will show the effective date of the current version.

14. Supervisory authority & complaints

If you are in Sri Lanka, you may contact the Personal Data Protection Authority (or other designated supervisory authority) to lodge a complaint. If you are in the EU, you may contact your local data protection authority. You retain the right to seek judicial remedies where permitted by law.
